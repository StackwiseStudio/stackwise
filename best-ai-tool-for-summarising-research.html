What to Look for in an AI Tool for Research Summarisation

1

Accuracy — No Hallucinations

The worst thing an AI summarisation tool can do is invent findings that aren't in the paper. Test any tool by asking it to summarise a paper you've already read — if it adds details that weren't there, don't trust it with papers you haven't read yet.

2

Citation and Source Tracking

A good summary tells you where in the paper each key finding came from. This lets you quickly jump to the relevant section for more detail without re-reading the entire document.

3

Long Document Support

Some AI tools struggle with documents over 20 pages. Research papers, reports, and whitepapers are often 40–100 pages long. Make sure the tool you choose can handle the full document without truncating it.

4

Output Format Options

Different use cases need different output formats. Sometimes you want a 3-sentence summary. Other times you need a structured breakdown with key findings, methodology, limitations, and conclusions.

Step-by-Step: How to Get Started

1

Upload or paste the paper — don't just give the title

For accurate summarisation, the AI needs to read the actual paper, not search for it. Upload the PDF directly or paste the full text. Asking an AI to summarise a paper by title risks it hallucinating details.

2

Ask for a structured summary, not just 'summarise this'

Specify the format you want: 'Give me the key finding in one sentence, the methodology in 2–3 sentences, the main limitations, and 3 practical implications.' Specific instructions produce far more useful output.

3

Verify 2–3 key claims against the original

Never trust an AI summary on important research without spot-checking. Pick 2–3 specific claims from the summary and verify them against the original paper. This takes 5 minutes and tells you whether you can trust the tool's output.

4

Ask follow-up questions to go deeper on specific sections

Once you have the summary, use the AI to drill into specific parts: 'Tell me more about the methodology they used in section 3' or 'What were the specific limitations the authors acknowledged?' This is faster than finding the section yourself.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can AI summarise research papers accurately enough to trust?

Yes — with caveats. The tools ranked here are grounded in the actual document and cite their sources. Always spot-check 2–3 claims before relying on a summary for important decisions. AI excels at saving reading time, not replacing critical analysis.

What file formats can these tools handle?

All three handle PDF (the most common research format). Perplexity can also summarise URLs directly. Claude can handle plain text, Word documents, and PDFs. Scholarcy is primarily PDF-focused.

Can these tools handle papers in languages other than English?

Perplexity and Claude both handle multilingual content well. Claude in particular maintains strong accuracy when summarising papers in French, Spanish, German, and several other languages. Scholarcy is primarily optimised for English.

How much do these tools cost for regular research use?

Perplexity Pro at $20/mo and Claude Pro at $20/mo both offer enough usage for daily research summarisation. Scholarcy at $9.99/mo is the most affordable specialist option. All three have free tiers worth trying before committing.
