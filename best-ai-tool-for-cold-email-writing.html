What to Look for in an AI Tool for Cold Email Writing

1

Personalisation Depth

A tool that writes the same opening line for every prospect will get ignored. Look for tools that pull in real data — company name, industry, recent news, job title — so each email feels written for that one person.

2

Tone and Length Control

Cold emails need to be short, direct, and conversational — ideally under 100 words. Many AI tools default to formal, wordy output. You need clear controls to keep the tone human and the length tight.

3

Sequence Support

One email rarely converts. The best tools let you build multi-step sequences — initial email, follow-up on day 3, follow-up on day 7 — with AI writing each step as a coherent thread.

4

Sending Infrastructure

Some tools just write the email; others include the sending, warm-up, and tracking too. If you're doing volume outreach, having everything in one place saves hours.

Step-by-Step: How to Get Started

1

Define your ideal customer before opening the AI

Write down exactly who you're targeting — job title, company size, and the one problem you solve for them. AI output is only as good as the context you give it. Vague input produces vague, generic emails that get deleted.

2

Give the AI a specific, detailed prompt

Describe your product, your prospect's role, and the exact pain point you address. The more specific — 'VP of Sales at B2B SaaS companies with 20–100 employees' — the more targeted and relevant the output.

3

Edit the output before sending — especially the opening line

AI gives you a strong first draft, not a finished email. Read it out loud. If it sounds robotic, rewrite the opening line in your own voice. The subject line should always be tweaked manually.

4

Build a 3-step sequence, not just one email

A single cold email gets a 1–3% reply rate. A 3-step sequence with follow-ups on day 3 and day 7 typically triples that. Use AI to write all three steps as a coherent thread.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can AI actually write cold emails that get replies?

Yes — but only if you give it enough context and edit the output. AI speeds up writing and beats blank-page paralysis. Treat it as a first-draft generator, not a finished-email machine. Always personalise the opening line yourself.

Will AI cold emails end up in spam?

Email content doesn't cause spam filtering — your sending domain reputation does. Use a tool like Instantly.ai that includes email warm-up, or warm your domain separately before sending at volume.

What's the best free AI tool for writing cold emails?

Copy.ai's free tier is the strongest free option — 2,000 words per month with dedicated cold email templates. Genuinely useful for small volumes. Once you need sequences, move to a paid plan.

How do I make AI cold emails sound less robotic?

Three things: rewrite the opening line with a specific real observation about the recipient. Keep the total email under 80 words. End with one low-friction question instead of a hard call to action. Short and human always beats long and polished in cold email.
