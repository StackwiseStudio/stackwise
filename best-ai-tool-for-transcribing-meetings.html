What to Look for in an AI Tool for Meeting Transcription

1

Transcription Accuracy

The number one metric. Test any tool on a real meeting with background noise, accents, and technical terminology before committing. Most tools quote 90%+ accuracy — but real-world accuracy varies significantly based on audio quality and speaker accents.

2

Speaker Identification

A transcript that says 'Speaker 1... Speaker 2...' throughout is only slightly more useful than no transcript at all. Look for tools that label speakers by name automatically, either through voice recognition training or by identifying speakers from calendar invites.

3

Action Item Extraction

The most valuable feature is automatic action item detection — the AI identifies commitments made in the meeting ('John will send the report by Friday') and creates a task list without anyone having to do it manually.

4

Calendar and Video Platform Integration

The best tools join your Zoom, Teams, or Google Meet calls automatically by reading your calendar — no manual setup before each meeting required.

Step-by-Step: How to Get Started

1

Connect your calendar on day one

The most important setup step is connecting your Google or Outlook calendar so the AI bot joins your meetings automatically. Without this, you'll forget to start the transcription manually on calls you weren't expecting to need.

2

Train speaker identification with short samples

In Otter.ai and Fireflies, spend 5 minutes recording yourself and each regular meeting participant saying a few sentences. This trains speaker identification to correctly label who said what in future meetings, dramatically improving transcript usefulness.

3

Review the AI summary immediately after the meeting

Open the summary within 30 minutes while the context is fresh. Correct any misidentified speakers or action items. This takes 5 minutes and ensures the record is accurate before anyone forgets what was actually decided.

4

Share the summary with all attendees the same day

Send the AI-generated summary and action item list to all meeting participants immediately. This replaces the manual follow-up email, confirms what was agreed, and creates accountability for assigned actions without any additional writing.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is AI meeting transcription really?

In good audio conditions with clear English speakers, 95%+ accuracy is achievable with Otter.ai. With heavy accents, technical jargon, or poor audio quality, accuracy drops to 80–90%. Always review the transcript for critical details rather than treating it as a verbatim legal record.

Will the other meeting participants know they're being recorded?

Most tools require you to inform participants when recording. Otter and Fireflies send a bot that participants can see in the call. Check your local laws on recording consent — in many jurisdictions you need to inform all parties that a meeting is being recorded.

Do these tools work with in-person meetings?

Yes — open the app on your phone and place it in the centre of the table. Otter.ai's mobile app transcribes in-person conversations well in quiet environments. Accuracy decreases with more speakers talking over each other.

Can I keep meeting transcripts private?

Yes — all three tools have private by default settings. You control who can access transcripts. Fireflies has enterprise-grade access controls for teams that need strict meeting confidentiality.
