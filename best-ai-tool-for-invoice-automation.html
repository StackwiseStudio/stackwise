What to Look for in an AI Tool for Invoice & Data Entry Automation

1

OCR Accuracy Across Invoice Formats

Invoices come in hundreds of different formats — PDFs, images, scanned documents, handwritten forms. The tool you choose needs to extract data accurately from all of them, not just clean, well-formatted PDFs. Test with your messiest invoices.

2

Accounting Software Integration

The whole value of automation is that data flows into your system automatically. Look for direct integration with the accounting software you already use — QuickBooks, Xero, Sage — so extracted data populates the right fields without copy-pasting.

3

Validation and Error Flagging

A tool that extracts data but doesn't flag when something looks wrong (duplicate invoice, unusual amount, missing PO number) creates a false sense of security. Error detection is what makes automation trustworthy.

4

Approval Workflow Support

For businesses where invoices need authorisation before payment, look for tools that route invoices to the right approver automatically and track approval status — replacing manual email chains with a proper workflow.

Step-by-Step: How to Get Started

1

Audit your current invoice processing workflow first

Before automating, map out how invoices currently arrive (email, post, supplier portal), who processes them, where data goes, and what your approval process looks like. Automation works best when you know exactly what you're replacing.

2

Start with one supplier's invoices to test accuracy

Don't automate everything on day one. Pick the supplier who sends the most invoices and run their last 20 through the tool. Check every extracted field against the original. Only when accuracy is confirmed should you expand to all suppliers.

3

Set up your accounting software integration and field mapping

Connect the tool to your accounting software and map the extracted fields to the right accounts — supplier name, invoice number, line items, tax amounts, due date. This setup takes 1–2 hours but eliminates manual data entry permanently after that.

4

Configure exception rules and approval routing

Set rules for what should be flagged for human review — amounts over a threshold, invoices without matching purchase orders, duplicate invoice numbers. Route these exceptions to the right person automatically rather than letting errors reach payment.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is AI invoice data extraction?

Modern AI invoice tools achieve 95–99% accuracy on clean, digital PDFs. Accuracy drops to 85–95% on scanned or handwritten documents. Always have a validation step for the first few months to catch the remaining errors before they reach your accounting system.

Can these tools handle invoices in different languages?

Dext and Rossum both handle multi-language invoices. Rossum in particular is used by large international businesses processing invoices in 30+ languages. If you receive invoices in languages other than English, test this specifically during your trial.

What happens to invoices the AI can't read?

All three tools have a fallback where unreadable documents are flagged for manual review. You set the confidence threshold — anything below 90% accuracy gets a human check. This gives you automation speed for clean documents and human oversight for the exceptions.

Will this replace my accounts payable staff?

It will dramatically reduce the time they spend on manual data entry — freeing them for exception handling, supplier relationships, and cash flow management. It replaces a task, not a person — the time savings typically go into higher-value work.
