What to Look for in an AI Tool for Social Media Content Creation

1

Multi-Platform Output

Each social platform has different character limits, tone expectations, and best practices. A tool that just generates generic text is less useful than one that formats content specifically for LinkedIn, Instagram, Twitter, and Facebook simultaneously.

2

Content Repurposing

Your best asset is content you've already created — blog posts, podcasts, videos, customer reviews. The best AI tools can turn a 2,000-word blog post into 15 social posts, saving far more time than creating from scratch.

3

Brand Voice Consistency

Social content that sounds different every week confuses followers. Look for tools that learn your brand voice and apply it consistently — so posts sound like they came from the same person, not a rotating roster of writers.

4

Scheduling Integration

Generating content and publishing it are separate steps. The best tools include direct scheduling to your social platforms so you can go from idea to published post without switching between multiple apps.

Step-by-Step: How to Get Started

1

Decide your content pillars before generating anything

Define 3–5 topic categories your brand always talks about — for example: industry tips, behind-the-scenes, customer stories, product features, and motivational content. AI generates better posts when it knows the context and purpose.

2

Give the AI your existing best posts as examples

Paste your top 5 best-performing posts into the brand voice settings. This teaches the AI your preferred tone, length, and style far more accurately than trying to describe it in words.

3

Generate a month of content in one session

Block 2 hours, sit down with your AI tool, and generate 30 days of posts across all your platforms. Review and edit as you go. Scheduling everything at once means you never have to think about it again until next month.

4

Track what performs and feed it back to the AI

After 30 days, look at which posts got the most engagement. Tell the AI: 'Write more posts like this one' — or use Lately's automatic learning feature to let it analyse your best performers automatically.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will followers know my social posts are AI-generated?

Not if you add a personal touch. AI provides the structure and draft; you add the specific detail, story, or opinion that makes it feel genuine. A post that starts 'Had an interesting conversation with a client this week about...' followed by AI-drafted insights reads completely authentically.

How many posts should a small business publish per week?

3–5 posts per week across your main platform is more effective than daily posting with inconsistent quality. Consistency beats frequency. AI makes 3–5 quality posts per week genuinely sustainable for a one-person business.

Can AI generate images for social posts too?

Yes — tools like Canva's AI, Adobe Firefly, and Midjourney generate social-ready images. Buffer and Hootsuite both integrate with Canva directly. A good workflow is: AI generates the copy, Canva generates or helps design the visual.

Is AI social content against platform terms of service?

No — none of the major platforms prohibit AI-assisted content. What they prohibit is spam, misleading content, and coordinated inauthentic behaviour. Thoughtful AI-assisted content is completely within the rules of every major platform.
