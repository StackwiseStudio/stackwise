What to Look for in an AI Tool for SEO Blog Post Writing

1

SEO Guidance Built In

The best tools don't just write — they guide. They show you which keywords to include, how often, which questions to answer, and how your article compares to the top-ranking competitors for your target term. Writing without this guidance is guessing.

2

SERP Analysis

Understanding what's already ranking for your target keyword tells you exactly what your article needs to include. Tools that analyse the top 10 results and extract their common topics give you a significant competitive advantage.

3

Output Quality — Not Just Quantity

Some AI tools produce fast, high-volume output that reads like it was written by a machine. For SEO, quality matters more than speed — a well-written 1,500 word article that engages readers beats a 3,000-word AI dump that no one finishes.

4

Plagiarism and AI Detection Risk

Google penalises content that appears to be low-quality AI spam. Look for tools that produce unique, naturally varied output and give you enough control to edit heavily. Run output through Originality.ai before publishing to check.

Step-by-Step: How to Get Started

1

Research your target keyword before writing a single word

Use Google's 'People also ask' section, Surfer or Frase's SERP analysis, or even just read the top 5 ranking articles. Know what questions your article needs to answer before the AI starts generating content.

2

Create a detailed brief for the AI to follow

Don't just type your keyword and click generate. Write a brief that specifies: target keyword, article length, key sections to cover, specific questions to answer, and tone. The more specific the brief, the better the output.

3

Generate section by section, not all at once

Generate the introduction, then each section, then the conclusion separately. Review and edit each section before moving to the next. This produces far better output than generating the entire 2,000-word article in one go.

4

Edit heavily before publishing — add your expertise

Add statistics, personal examples, specific advice from your own experience, and opinions. This is what makes your article different from the 100 other AI-generated articles on the same topic. Google rewards originality and depth.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will Google penalise AI-written blog posts?

Google penalises low-quality, unhelpful content — not AI-generated content specifically. An AI-assisted article that is well-researched, genuinely useful, and heavily edited to add original insight will rank. A thin AI dump that offers nothing new will not.

How long should SEO blog posts be?

For most topics, 1,200–2,000 words is the sweet spot. For competitive or complex topics, 2,500–4,000 words may be needed to outrank established competitors. Check the length of the top 3 ranking articles for your target keyword and aim to match or exceed them.

Do I need to know SEO to use these tools?

Basic understanding helps — knowing what a keyword is, what search intent means, and why internal links matter. But tools like Frase explain their recommendations in plain English, making them accessible even to SEO beginners.

How many articles per month do I need to see results?

Consistency matters more than volume. Two high-quality, well-optimised articles per week outperforms six rushed, thin articles. Most businesses see meaningful organic traffic growth within 3–6 months of consistent quality publishing.
